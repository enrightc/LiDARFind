What is LiDAR

Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) is a remote sensing technology that uses laser pulses to create detailed 3D maps of the Earth's surface. By measuring the time it takes for laser light to bounce back from the ground, LiDAR can produce highly accurate models of terrain and surface features.

Archaeologists can use LiDAR to reveal subtle ground changes, exposing historical sites that may not be visible to the naked eye. This powerful tool allows researchers to discover and map extensive archaeological sites quickly, even in challenging environments such as beneath trees.

Every discovery you make and save contributes to a comprehensive map of Wales' hidden archaeological treasures.
